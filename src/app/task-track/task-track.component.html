Conflict of Interest Guidelines

 

Conflict of Interest (COI) refers to any situation where an employee’s personal interest might conflict with the interests of any entity comprising part of the Majid Al Futtaim group of companies (such entity, the “Company”, and such group, the “Group”). This is an unwanted circumstance as it may improperly influence or could be seen as improperly influencing the employee’s judgement, decisions, actions and commitment to act, at all times, in the best interests of the Company and the Group. Such unwanted circumstances can also negatively impact the Company’s, and consequently, the Group’s reputation.

 

The basic principle is that each employee must act in a way which does not and will not adversely affect the reputation, business interests or integrity of the Company or the Group. The employee should not benefit directly or indirectly beyond what is provided to the employee in their employment package.

 

The objective of these guidelines is to ensure transparency by requiring all employees to disclose any and all personal interests with any party having a business relationship with the Group in order to take remedial actions when needed.

 

Definitions

Business Partners refer to companies having business relationships with and/or provide products and services to any Company within the Group.

Employees  are defined as all persons who are employed by any Company within the Group on a full-time, part-time, or contract basis.

Family Members or Relatives are defined to include spouse, children, grandchildren, parents, grandparents, brothers and sisters (including half-brothers and half-sisters), brothers and sisters-in-law, parents-in-law, children-in-law, uncles, aunts and first cousins of the employee. If any interest is present in a relative or a family member, that interest will be considered to be that of the employee.

 

Scope 

This COI guidelines apply to all Employees and their Family Members in the UAE and all other countries where the Group operates or intends to operate. 

 

Key Principles

  • No senior officers or Employees (or their Family Members or Relatives) shall derive any personal profit, gain or benefit, directly or indirectly, by reason of his or her employment with the Group. 
  • Each Employee shall identify and disclose to the Company any and all personal interests which he or she (or their Family Members or Relatives), directly or indirectly, have with any Business Partner having a relationship with any Company within the Group, including but not limited to contractors, consultants, suppliers, business partners and tenants.
  • Any personal/romantic relationships with direct reports, line managers, any other Employees and/or a Business Partner which may be perceived as impairing or adversely affecting an Employee’s judgement to make business decisions objectively must be promptly disclosed for appropriate safeguards to be introduced.
  • If an Employee becomes aware of a potential conflict of interest situation, the Employee should immediately notify his/her direct manager and the relevant Company compliance team. Actions may be taken to remove the party from further participation in Majid Al Futtaim-related activities until the conflict of interest is reviewed or a waiver granted and legal advice obtained.
  • On an annual basis all Employees must complete the below Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form. Employees shall immediately update and resubmit this form, if an individual's financial, business, or employment relationship changes throughout the year and a conflict of interest could exist.
  • All conflict of interest disclosures shall be analysed by management and risk mitigation actions be implemented to protect the Company and the concerned Employee, where needed.

Gifts, Hospitality and Entertainment

 

  • There are situations involving an exchange of Gifts, Hospitality and Entertainment (GHE), which can be perceived as a conflict of interest. GHE must be carried out in full compliance with Majid Al Futtaim  GHE Policy .
  • If you haven’t registered GHE received using the GHE tool , please use the below Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form to disclose them.

 

Examples of non-allowable gifts, benefits or relationships

  • Cash payments
  • Travel tickets and/or trips, both local and international
  • Accommodations
  • Vacations
  • Tickets to musical or sporting events (unless such tickets are provided to the Company as part of a sponsorship program by the Company)
  • Lavish meals exceeding the limit specified below
  • Heavily discounted or free products and services from any Business Partner
  • Direct and indirect sponsorship of social, philanthropist and sporting events for self, family members and/or team
  • Expensive promotional items (exceeding allowable monetary limit)
  • Providing consulting services to any Business Partners 
  • Promise of future employment by any Business Partner (for self or Family Members or Relatives)
  • Being a salaried employee or receiving any form or compensation (monetary or otherwise) from any person or entity outside the Group
  • Director or board member position in a company that is in direct competition with any of the Group entities.
  • Employment of Family Members or Relatives by any Business Partner

Potential conflict of interest requiring special approval 

  • Employment of Family Members or Relatives within the Group
  • Serving on the Board of Directors or advisory committees of any company that is NOT in direct competition with the Company or Group
  • Personal investment in any Business Partner or companies in direct competition with any Group entity

Samples of allowable gifts or benefits:

  • Business meals (not to exceed AED 2,000 in total cumulative value per annum or the lower threshold set by your OpCo & country of operation from a single source)
  • Promotional items (not to exceed AED 500 in total cumulative value per annum or the lower threshold set by your OpCo & country of operation from a single source)

Compliance with the Guidelines:

  • Compliance with the COI guidelines is mandatory. Employees are ultimately accountable to fully disclose any potential or actual conflict of interest. The existence of any actual or potential conflict of interest is not a violation by itself. Failure to timely disclose is considered a violation and could lead to disciplinary actions including termination.
